## Plain-Language Summary

The full policy is below, but here's the short version of what WavePoint does with your information:

- We collect the minimum we need to give you access to our indicators, process your subscription, and support you.
- We use trusted processors (Stripe for payments, Supabase for the database, Rewardful for affiliate tracking, Cloudflare for hosting) — we do not sell your data.
- If you were referred by a partner, we share attribution data with that partner. Your email is anonymized until you become a paying subscriber.
- You can access, export, correct, or delete your data at any time.
- EU, UK, and California users have additional specific rights described in §7.

## 3. How We Use Your Information

We use personal information for the following purposes:

- **Service delivery** — authenticate you, provision your indicator access on NinjaTrader or other platforms, maintain your profile and subscription
- **Billing and payments** — process subscriptions, renewals, refunds, and tax calculations through Stripe
- **Support** — respond to inquiries, troubleshoot, provide account assistance
- **Communications** — send transactional messages (receipts, service updates, policy changes, security alerts) and, where you have opted in, product and marketing communications
- **Fraud prevention and security** — detect account sharing, machine-ID resets outside normal patterns, payment fraud, and abuse of the Services
- **Partner program operation** — calculate commissions, process partner payouts, provide partners with (anonymized and de-anonymized) referral performance data as described in §4.2
- **Analytics and product improvement** — understand how the Services are used in aggregate to improve features, performance, and content
- **Legal compliance** — comply with applicable laws including tax, accounting, anti-money-laundering, and lawful requests from authorities

We do not use your actual trading activity (positions, P&L, broker account data) for any purpose — we do not collect it.

### 4.2 Referral Partners

If you came to us through one of our partners' referral links, we share a limited, purpose-built set of information with that partner. Our partner agreements include a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) restricting how partners may use this data.

**What partners see before you become a paying subscriber:**

- An anonymized representation of your email address (e.g., `j***@e***.com`)
- The date you clicked their referral link, started a trial, and (if applicable) converted
- Your subscription tier, if you have one
- No NinjaTrader Machine ID, TradingView username, IP address, payment information, or usage data

**What partners see once you become a paying subscriber:**

- Your full email address — to enable legitimate customer-support, relationship-management, and commission-audit purposes
- Your subscription tier and lifecycle status (active, paused, canceled)
- No NinjaTrader Machine ID, TradingView username, IP address, payment information, or indicator usage data

**What partners never see:**

- Your trading activity, broker account information, or the actual use of indicators
- Other customers not referred by them
- The identities of customers who have opted out of partner visibility (see §7.1)

You may opt out of partner visibility at any time by contacting [privacy@wavepointanalytics.com](mailto:privacy@wavepointanalytics.com) or using the relevant setting in your portal. Opting out does not affect your subscription or access.

## 5. Cookies and Similar Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies (such as local storage and pixels) for the purposes below.

| Category | Purpose | Can Be Disabled? |
|---|---|---|
| Strictly necessary | Authentication, session management, CSRF protection, security | No — the Services will not function without these |
| Functional | User preferences (theme, display options) | Yes, but may degrade experience |
| Analytics | Aggregated usage statistics | Yes — disabled by default in the EU/EEA/UK until you grant consent |
| Affiliate attribution | Rewardful referral tracking; typically 60-day window | Yes — users may opt out; disabling may prevent the referring partner from receiving credit for a conversion |

**Cookie consent for EU/EEA/UK users:** On your first visit, you will be shown a consent banner. Non-essential cookies (analytics, marketing, and optional partner attribution cookies) are not set until you grant consent. You can change your preferences at any time via the "Cookie Preferences" link in the site footer.

**Do Not Track:** Some browsers transmit "Do Not Track" signals. There is no industry consensus on how to interpret these signals, and we currently do not respond to them. We treat all EU/EEA/UK users as having an active opt-out for non-essential cookies until they grant consent through our banner.

## 7. Your Rights

### 7.1 Rights Available to All Users

Regardless of where you live, you may:

- **Access** the personal information we hold about you
- **Correct** inaccurate or incomplete information
- **Delete** your account and associated personal information (subject to the retention obligations in §6)
- **Export** your data in a commonly used, machine-readable format
- **Opt out of marketing** communications at any time by clicking "unsubscribe" in any marketing email or contacting us directly
- **Opt out of partner visibility** — request that your referral attribution be severed from the originating partner

### 7.2 EU / EEA / UK Users (GDPR and UK GDPR)

In addition to §7.1, you have the right to:

- **Object** to processing based on our legitimate interests
- **Restrict** processing in specified circumstances
- **Withdraw consent** where processing is based on consent (this does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal)
- **Lodge a complaint** with your supervisory authority (for example, the UK Information Commissioner's Office, or the data-protection authority in your EU member state)

**Legal bases for processing (GDPR Art. 6):**

- **Contract** — account creation, service delivery, billing, support
- **Legitimate interests** — fraud prevention, account-sharing detection, product improvement, partner commission calculation (balanced against your rights and freedoms)
- **Consent** — marketing communications, non-essential cookies, optional analytics
- **Legal obligation** — tax, accounting, anti-fraud compliance, responses to lawful authority requests

### 7.3 California Users (CCPA / CPRA)

California residents have the right to:

- Know what personal information we collect and how it is used and disclosed
- Request deletion of personal information, subject to exceptions
- Correct inaccurate personal information
- Opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information (we do not sell, and we do not share for cross-context behavioral advertising)
- Limit the use and disclosure of sensitive personal information (we do not collect sensitive personal information as defined by CPRA except where clearly needed for service provision)
- Non-discrimination for exercising these rights

**Categories of personal information collected in the past 12 months** (CCPA categories):
Identifiers, commercial information, internet or electronic network activity, geolocation (IP-level only), professional information (for partner applications). Sources, purposes, and recipients for each category are described throughout this policy.

### 7.4 How to Exercise Your Rights

Send requests to [privacy@wavepointanalytics.com](mailto:privacy@wavepointanalytics.com). We may verify your identity before acting on a request. We will respond within the timelines required by applicable law — generally 30 days (EU/UK), 45 days (California), and as soon as reasonably practicable elsewhere.

For account deletion, you may also use the **"Delete Account"** button in your user portal (Settings → Account).

## 9. Data Security

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures designed to protect personal information, including:

- TLS (HTTPS) encryption for all data in transit
- Encryption at rest for database storage (Supabase AES-256)
- Row-Level Security (RLS) policies enforcing least-privilege access to database records
- Role-based access controls for internal staff
- Audit logging of administrative actions
- Secrets management for API keys and credentials
- Regular review of access, configurations, and dependencies

No security measure is perfect. If we become aware of a security incident that is reasonably likely to result in risk to your rights, we will notify you and the relevant authorities in accordance with applicable law.

## 14. Lawyer Review Notes

> This section should be removed before publication. It documents items that need legal input and judgment calls the founders should weigh in on.

1. **Entity and mailing address** — confirm the exact legal name and registered mailing address for all references.
2. **EU representative (Article 27 GDPR)** — because the Services will be offered to users in the EU, an EU representative is almost certainly required unless the "occasional processing" exception clearly applies. Services like Prighter, EDPO, or GDPR-Rep.eu provide this for roughly $100–$400/year and will be named in the policy.
3. **UK representative (Article 27 UK GDPR)** — same analysis for the UK. The same providers usually offer bundled EU+UK representation.
4. **DPA template for partners** — the policy references a Data Processing Agreement with partners. An actual DPA template (or joint-controller agreement, depending on structure) must be drafted and executed with each partner before the partner portal goes live.
5. **DPAs with processors** — confirm DPAs (or equivalent) are in place with Stripe, Supabase, Rewardful, Cloudflare, Google, and Web3Forms. Most offer standard DPAs via their websites.
6. **Sub-processor list publication** — GDPR expects controllers to make the processor/sub-processor list available on request. The table in §4.1 largely satisfies this but should be kept current.
7. **Retention periods** — validated the 7-year billing-records figure against US federal tax obligations; confirm against any state-specific requirements for the states where users and partners are located.
8. **Sensitive personal information under CPRA** — confirm we do not process any categories (such as precise geolocation beyond IP-inferred region, financial account info other than last-4 via Stripe) that would trigger CPRA sensitive-data obligations.
9. **California Notice at Collection** — California requires a specific pre-collection notice describing categories and purposes. This can be satisfied by linking to this Privacy Policy at every point of collection; confirm implementation.
10. **"Shine the Light" (California Civil Code §1798.83)** — California users may request information about disclosures to third parties for direct marketing purposes. We do not make such disclosures; add a specific statement to that effect if counsel recommends.
11. **Payment-card surcharge or state-specific disclosures** — some states require specific billing disclosures for subscription products. Verify.
12. **Financial-services positioning** — WavePoint sells trading indicators, not investment advice. Reinforce this distinction in the Terms of Service and marketing materials. It may be worth adding a brief disclaimer in the Privacy Policy that we do not provide investment advice and do not collect brokerage account data.
13. **Automated decision-making (GDPR Art. 22)** — confirm none of our systems produce "legal or similarly significant" effects. The fraud-detection and account-sharing-detection systems are close to the line — in practice a human reviews before any account is suspended, but this workflow must be documented.
14. **Terms of Service** — this Privacy Policy must be paired with a Terms of Service document covering subscription terms, refund policy, indicator IP ownership, acceptable use, dispute resolution, arbitration/class-action waiver, governing law. Separate deliverable.
15. **Cookie banner implementation** — the policy references a cookie consent banner; this must be built and configured (likely via a lightweight tool like CookieYes, Osano, or a self-hosted open-source solution) and tested before EU-facing launch.
