## 19. Lawyer Review Notes

> This section should be removed before publication. It documents items that need legal input and judgment calls the founders should weigh in on.

### 19.1 Critical decisions to make before sign-off

1. **Refund window length (§4.6)** — 7, 14, 30 days? Shorter reduces abuse but creates friction; longer is customer-friendly but drives higher refund rates on a trading product. **Note:** This is the single most consequential customer-facing commitment. Must align with partner-side clawback window (per Board Briefing §6.1.1).
2. **Free trial length (§4.2)** — the Board Briefing assumes 14 days. Confirm.
3. **Free trial abuse handling (§4.2)** — is "one per household/IP/payment method" enforceable and acceptable, or is "one per verified identity" better? Anti-abuse vs. false-positive tradeoff.
4. **Governing law & venue (§17.6)** — based on state of formation (WavePoint LLC filed where?). Likely Delaware or Washington; counsel to advise.
5. **Arbitration provider (§17.2)** — JAMS vs. AAA. Both standard; JAMS is often more business-friendly, AAA is more consumer-familiar.
6. **Liability cap formula (§15.2)** — "greater of trailing 12-month fees paid or $100" is standard for SaaS. For a trading-adjacent product, counsel may recommend a fixed cap or a reduced multiplier given heightened liability exposure.
7. **Pricing commitments on renewal (§4.5)** — 30-day notice is standard; check if any state regulations require longer.

### 19.2 Regulatory positioning items

8. **CFTC / NFA status** — WavePoint provides indicators, not advice. But if indicators generate specific BUY/SELL signals, the CFTC may view this as investment advice requiring CTA (Commodity Trading Advisor) registration. Counsel should confirm:
   - Whether WavePoint needs to register as a CTA with the NFA
   - Whether the "Publishers' Exemption" applies (it often does for general-audience indicator products, but narrows if products are personalized or paired with advice)
   - How indicator marketing copy should be worded to stay in the exemption
9. **SEC posture** — less relevant for pure futures, but if any product touches securities (including cryptocurrency tokens deemed securities), additional disclosures may be required.
10. **State-level financial regulations** — some states (e.g., Texas, California) have additional disclosure requirements for trading-adjacent products.
11. **FTC Endorsement & Testimonial rules** — when partners publish testimonials or reviews, they must disclose the affiliate relationship. Our Partner Agreement should require this; counsel to approve language.
12. **Hypothetical/Simulated Performance (§8.4)** — NFA rules require specific disclosure language ("CFTC Rule 4.41") for hypothetical performance. If we ever show backtested results, the exact required language must appear. Add if we plan to.

### 19.3 International items

13. **EU/UK consumer rights** — EU Consumer Rights Directive gives consumers a 14-day withdrawal right for digital services, with specific opt-in language required to begin performance before the period expires. Must be incorporated.
14. **UK "Digital Content" rights** — Consumer Rights Act 2015 requires that digital content be "of satisfactory quality, fit for purpose, and as described." Cannot be disclaimed.
15. **Australian Consumer Law** — cannot disclaim consumer guarantees. Separate language likely required.
16. **Canadian CASL** — if we ever send marketing emails to Canadian addresses, CASL opt-in requirements apply.
17. **Automatic renewal disclosures** — California (SB-313), New York, and several EU jurisdictions require specific automatic-renewal disclosures at checkout and in reminder emails. May require specific checkbox language at signup.

### 19.4 Drafting items to confirm

18. **Entity name and registered address** — confirm exact legal name and mailing address across this doc and the Privacy Policy.
19. **Contact email aliases** — `hello@`, `support@`, `privacy@`, `legal@`, `dmca@` — confirm all are provisioned and monitored before launch.
20. **Rewardful's impact on refund mechanics** — when a customer receives a refund, Rewardful auto-adjusts the partner commission. Confirm this mechanism works the way we describe it in §5.
21. **"Free Access Seats" liability** — is a partner who grants a free seat to a friend creating any liability for us if that friend loses money trading? Minimal, but the ToS should bind free-seat users just like paid users. Language in §5.3 starts this; counsel to strengthen if needed.
22. **AI-generated content disclaimer** — as we incorporate AI into marketing copy, consider a disclosure similar to what media companies are adopting. Not urgent but worth considering.
23. **Export control** — US OFAC sanctions list; we should block users from sanctioned jurisdictions (Iran, North Korea, Syria, etc.). Implementation: Stripe Radar + country block at checkout.
24. **Dispute resolution clause enforceability under the FAA** — recent Supreme Court rulings have narrowed some arbitration carve-outs. Counsel to confirm current best practice.
25. **Cross-references to Privacy Policy and Partner DPA** — all three documents should be consistent in terminology and commitments. Check for drift during revision.
