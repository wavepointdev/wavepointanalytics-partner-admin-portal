Design notes

Admin Portal notes

Overview

This is the Internal Admin Portal — Founders view. Jason, George, and Derek each have full admin access. The orange banner at the top and orange accent on the user chip are deliberate visual markers that this is a privileged-access surface. Every action on this portal is logged to the audit trail.

Data sources: Stripe (revenue, customers, tax), Rewardful (partner attribution, commissions), Supabase (user identities, platform IDs, overrides, audit log, risk signals).

1
"Founders view" — role model

Today there are only 3 admin roles, all of them founders with full access. Future hire roles (e.g., "Support Agent" who can see users but not grant lifetime access, or "Analyst" who gets read-only BI access) are modeled but not yet active. RLS policies are written with future roles in mind — adding a new role won't require a schema change.

2
System status strip — critical, not optional

The four traffic lights represent the infrastructure paths that, if they break, cost you money silently. Stripe webhooks failing → customers pay but don't get access, or cancel but still have access. Rewardful sync breaking → commissions miscalculated. NinjaTrader delivery failing → paying customer with no product. A yellow or red light here is a P1 operational incident, not a dashboard curiosity. We also send an email + Discord alert for any red state.

3
Churn: watchable, not alarming

3.2% monthly churn is typical for a prosumer SaaS at this price point (industry avg 3-5%). The +0.4pp movement is shown in warning orange, not red — it's a signal to watch, not a fire. Clicking into this KPI would open a detail view with churn by plan tier, by cohort, by reason code.

4
Revenue recognition — the important one

Booked MRR is what Stripe shows you gross. Net MRR subtracts an estimated refund liability (based on historical refund rate × last 30 days of new revenue) and chargeback reserves. The gap between these two numbers is what's actually yours to spend vs. what might claw back. This is the single biggest source of "we thought we were bigger than we were" surprises for early-stage SaaS — surfacing it prevents that.

5
Product mix · multi-platform ready

Currently NinjaTrader-only. The "Coming in phase 2" line is a real architectural commitment — the data model distinguishes platform from product. When TradingView launches, this panel grows a platform filter and starts showing that split. No rework needed.

6
Partner leaderboard — internal only

Partners cannot see this ranking or each other's data. This is purely internal. Clicking a partner row opens their detailed profile: lifetime performance, commission history, seat quota, coupon settings, payout schedule, contract terms, communication log. Manual override tools (adjust commission, bump quota, extend clawback, suspend) live here too.

7
Geographic Top 5 — simple by design

A table, not a heatmap. At 5,000 users, a US-wide heatmap just shows California, New York, Texas, and Florida. Not useful. This simple top-5 with a link to the full Geographic detail view (with US-state breakdown, VAT/sales-tax status, per-region MRR) is more actionable. The detail page is where you'd go if you're planning a state-specific compliance review or a regional campaign.

8
Risk alerts — four flavors

Four categories of risk signal we track:
• Chargebacks & disputes — real money, highest priority
• Account sharing — multi-IP, multi-country, rapid-session patterns
• Machine ID reset abuse — exceeding the 1/30-day limit
• Coupon velocity anomaly — a code being used at 10×+ its historical rate may indicate it leaked publicly

The thresholds for each are admin-configurable. We err on the side of "surface and let a human decide" rather than auto-suspend, because wrongly suspending a paying customer is way worse than letting an actual abuser run for another day while a human reviews.

9
Audit log — immutable

Every admin action that affects a customer, partner, or financial record is logged with who, what, when, why. Writes are append-only — even admins cannot delete or edit audit entries (enforced at the RLS policy level). This protects you three ways: against a compromised admin account, against an internal dispute ("who made this change?"), and against external compliance requests.

Other screens in this portal
Customers
Search, filter, and drill into any customer. Shows subscription, billing history, platform IDs, IP history, support tickets, partner attribution, manual override buttons (grant lifetime access, extend trial, force ID reset, refund).
Revenue
MRR waterfall, cohort retention heatmap, recognition detail, plan-tier breakdowns, expansion/contraction tracking, per-product MRR, year-over-year growth.
Products
Full SKU catalog, adoption per product, pricing experiments, version control, platform delivery status, product-SKU ↔ commission-rate mapping.
Partners
All partners list, onboarding queue, per-partner profile + performance + settings, commission rate adjustments, seat quota management, suspension/termination flow, payout approval queue.
Risk
Full alert queue with filters, chargeback workflow (upload evidence, track dispute), account-sharing investigation tools, machine-ID reset history, fraud scoring, suspension workflow.
Operations
Webhook delivery log, sync job history, infrastructure health, feature flags, content access log (every indicator grant/revoke event), background job status.
Audit Log
Full immutable audit log with filters by actor, action type, target, date range. Exportable for compliance/investigation purposes. No edits, no deletes — ever.
Settings
Admin roles & users, global thresholds (refund window, clawback period, risk alert thresholds, coupon caps, seat-quota defaults), tax settings, email templates, feature flags.
Open questions for board
  • Admin role tiers — when you hire, who gets what level of access? Current mockup assumes 3 founders-only; needs a defined role matrix for future hires.
  • Risk alert thresholds — when is an account-sharing flag triggered (N IPs in M hours, M countries)? Too tight = noise, too loose = missed fraud.
  • Lifetime access ceiling — should there be a numeric cap on how many free lifetime grants can exist company-wide? Protects against "oops, we gave away 50 subs over 2 years".
  • Chargeback handling SLA — who owns responding to disputes? Stripe gives you ~7-21 days. Need a defined owner.
  • Cohort retention tracking — shown only as a link on this dashboard. Should it be a KPI card on page 1 too?
  • Founder notification preferences — what alerts should page each founder? (Chargeback → Jason? Partner issues → George? etc.)
  • External BI tool integration — do we want to export this data to Metabase or a similar tool once we've got real volumes, or keep BI in-portal?
  • Discord/email alerts integration — should system status alerts post to Discord? To a specific channel? With what level of detail?
Manual override toolkit

Not shown on this dashboard but available from any customer or partner detail page. All actions are logged in the audit trail and may require a reason string before committing.

  • Grant lifetime access — comp account, advisor pass, giveaway winner
  • Extend trial — "trial had a bug, give them another 14 days"
  • Force machine-ID reset — override the 1-per-30-day limit for support cases
  • Issue refund — triggers Stripe refund + automatic commission clawback
  • Adjust partner commission — retroactive correction, signing bonus, clawback waiver
  • Bump partner seat quota — upgrade tier, custom arrangement
  • Suspend partner — rule violation, contract termination
  • Revoke free seat — (mirrors the partner-side revoke, but admin can do cross-partner)
  • Resend webhook — manually replay a Stripe event if sync broke
