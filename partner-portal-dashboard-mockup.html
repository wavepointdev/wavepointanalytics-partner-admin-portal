Design notes

What you're looking at

Overview

This is the Partner Portal Dashboard — Owner view. A partner is a content creator who refers customers to WavePoint. The Owner is the senior person at that partner business; Team Members see a stripped-down version of this same screen (commission dollars hidden).

The data layer is Rewardful for attribution and commissions + Supabase for team management, support tickets, toolkit metadata, and anonymization.

1
Team nav — Owner only

The "Team" nav item appears only for the Owner role. Team Members don't see this link at all. Role is stored on the partner-user mapping table in Supabase and enforced via RLS.

2
Partner context in header

Partner name + tenure gives the partner a sense of identity inside a shared platform. The "Welcome back, Marcus" is the viewer's name, not the partner's — a small but important distinction in multi-user tenants.

3
Refund hold window

The 30-day clawback window is the single most important number in this system for partner trust. Whatever the board decides (30, 45, 60 days), it should be surfaced prominently so partners are never surprised. Rewardful stores the dollar amount; we compute the release date.

4
Payout detail drill-in

Clicking this opens a payout detail page with every individual commission line that makes up the payout. This is the single biggest friction-remover for partner trust — they can answer "why was my payout $1,842 and not $2,000?" themselves instead of emailing support.

5
Product-SKU breakdown

The chart shows conversions per indicator, so partners see which products their audience actually buys. This replaces Gemini's "niche heatmap" which doesn't apply to a futures-only product. When TradingView products launch, we can add a platform filter (NinjaTrader / TradingView).

6
Anonymization rule

Partners see anonymized emails (j***@g***.com) for trial/visit activity, and full emails once a user becomes a paying subscriber. Row 5 (refunded) is a judgment call — we currently un-mask refunded users since the partner takes a clawback; the board should confirm. Users can opt out of partner visibility entirely per the privacy policy.

7
Paid email revealed

mike.thompson@gmail.com is visible because he's a paid subscriber. The partner can now contact him for legitimate support and relationship reasons only, subject to the Partner DPA.

8
Coupon codes with guardrails

Partners can self-serve coupon codes. Guardrails are enforced on creation and shown in the header strip: max discount (default 25%), auto-expiry (90 days), max uses per code (50), and max concurrent active codes (3). All values are admin-configurable per-partner from the admin portal — so a large content creator can be given higher ceilings than a smaller one. Each create/deactivate event syncs to Rewardful and logs to Supabase for audit.

9
Free Access Seats — new concept

Each partner gets an admin-configurable quota of free access seats (default 5). A seat grants full indicator access to a named email without creating a Stripe customer. Used for live demos, giveaways, reviewer accounts, or high-value testimonial seeds. Revoking a seat cuts access instantly and refills the quota — so partners can rotate seats through their community. Every grant and revoke is logged for the admin audit trail. Admin can also set seats to auto-expire (e.g., 30 days) as a separate policy.

Other screens in this portal
Conversions
Full paginated referral table with filters (date range, status, product, platform). Export to CSV.
Commissions (Owner only)
Historical payout list, downloadable invoices/statements, payout method settings (PayPal/Wise/Stripe), 1099 info.
Referred Users
Dedicated search/filter view of referred users with the anonymization rules applied.
Marketing Toolkit
Full asset browser backed by Cloudflare R2 — logos, banners, swipe copy, disclosure templates, B-roll.
Support
Ticket list + submission form. Supabase-backed. Status: open / in progress / resolved.
Team (Owner only)
Invite by email, assign role (Owner / Team Member), remove, activity log.
Settings
Profile, notifications, payout details (Owner only), DPA/agreement history, 2FA.
Team Member view differs from this by:
  • No commission dollar amounts anywhere — the "Commissions" column in the referrals table shows a lock icon instead
  • No "Upcoming Payout" card
  • The 3 KPI cards related to money ($ Lifetime, $ Available, $ In Hold) are replaced with conversion counts
  • No "Team" nav item
  • Settings is read-only except for personal profile fields
Open questions for board
  • Refund hold window length (30 / 45 / 60 days) — visible in KPI #3
  • Anonymization for refunded users — reveal or keep anonymized?
  • Commission rate display — show "30% for life" prominently, or keep contractual and only show dollar outcomes?
  • Minimum payout threshold — do we hold until $50, or pay any amount?
  • Coupon max discount — current mockup shows 25% cap. Too generous or about right?
  • Default free seat quota — 5 is a guess. Should this scale with partner tier (e.g., 3 / 5 / 10)?
  • Free seat auto-expiry — permanent until revoked, or auto-expire after 30/60/90 days?
  • Live feed for team awareness — small panel showing "Someone just converted!" style activity? Some partners love this, some find it noisy. Not in this mockup.
